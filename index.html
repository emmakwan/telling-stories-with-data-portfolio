Hi! I'm Emma, and this is my portfolio for 94-470 Telling Stories With Data at CMU.

About Me

My pronouns are she/her/hers. I am a junior at Carnegie Mellon studying Information Systems. For those who aren't as familiar with Information Systems, the program involves studying the intersection of people, technology, and processes. I am personally very interested in this intersection. In the past, I've interned at Bank of America, worked with Mattel Creations for their product marketing strategy, and am currently interning as a Product Intern at TutorMe. Outside of work and school, I enjoy watching TV shows, reading books (I read 56 books in 2021! My favorites included Long Division by Kiese Laymon and Anxious People by Frederik Backman.), and playing Super Mario 3D World on my Switch.

What I Hope To Learn

I've always been really intrigued by data and data visualizations! One of my favorite publications is The New York Times's The Upshot, which focuses on data-centric journalism. Although I have some basic experience with visualizing data as I worked as a volunteer data analyst on two progressive political campaigns as part of the organization Bluebonnet Data, I hope to learn how to level up my skills by learning more about color theory, different types of visualization options, and using new software like Tableau. I love the idea of being able to tell full and interesting stories through data and want to get more comfortable working with big datasets. One of the most important skills that I also hope to improve on is my ability to critique data visualizations and identify what aspects of a visualization make it work versus detracting from its goal. Through Telling Stories With Data, I hope to accomplish these learning goals and more while working with my awesome group of classmates!
